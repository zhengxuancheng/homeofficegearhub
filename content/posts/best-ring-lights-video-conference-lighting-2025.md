## FAQ

### Where should I place my ring light for video calls?

The ideal position is directly behind your monitor, centered on your webcam lens, at roughly eye level. This creates catchlights in your eyes (makes you look engaged) and fills in shadows from overhead lighting. If the light is too close to your face, it washes you out. If it's too far, it doesn't help. About 18–24 inches from your face is the sweet spot for most ring lights.

### Can I use a ring light in a room with natural window light?

Yes, but match the color temperature. Ring lights with adjustable color temps (3000K–6500K) can match window daylight (around 5600K) or warm office lighting (around 4000K). If your ring light is daylight and your room has warm bulbs, you'll look cool-toned against a warm background. Set the ring light to the same temperature as your ambient light.

### Is a ring light the same as a softbox?

No. Ring lights produce a circular catchlight reflection in the eyes (the ring reflection you see in influencer photos), while softboxes produce a rectangle or octagon catchlight. For video calls, a ring light is simpler to set up and takes less space. Softboxes produce softer, more even light but require a stand and more desk real estate.

### Do I need a ring light if my webcam has built-in low light correction?

Built-in webcam low light correction works by boosting the ISO, which introduces digital noise and flattens the image. A dedicated ring light lets your webcam run at its native ISO — meaning sharper video, more natural skin tones, and no grain. Any webcam, even a $50 one, looks significantly better with proper lighting.
