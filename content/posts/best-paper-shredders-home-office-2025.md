A paper shredder is one of those things you don't need until you do. Then you have a stack of bank statements, old tax returns, and junk mail with your address on it, and you either shred it or risk someone going through your trash.

But not all shredders are the same. Some handle 6 sheets at a time and overheat after 2 minutes. Others chew through 16 sheets continuously for half an hour. We tested 6 paper shredders for home offices, from basic cross-cut models to high-security micro-cut machines, and evaluated shred quality, speed, noise, and reliability.

## What to Look For

### Security Level

Cross-cut (P-3) turns paper into strips about 1/8 x 1 inch. Fine enough for most personal documents. Micro-cut (P-4 or P-5) reduces paper to confetti about 1/32 x 5/16 inch, making reassembly effectively impossible. For home office use with bank statements and credit card offers, P-3 or P-4 is sufficient. P-5 is overkill unless you handle sensitive business documents.

### Sheet Capacity

The rated sheet count is the maximum the shredder can handle at once. A 6-8 sheet capacity is fine for personal use — you shred a few pages at a time. 10-12 sheets is better for small offices. Higher capacity models are faster for large jobs but bigger and louder.

### Run Time / Cool Down

Entry-level shredders run for 2-3 minutes before needing a 20-30 minute cool-down. Better models run for 10-20 minutes continuously. If you regularly shred more than a few pages at a time, a longer run time is worth paying for.

### Jam Prevention

Nothing is more frustrating than a shredded jam halfway through a document. Look for models with auto-reverse (reverses the blades when a jam is detected) and a wide feed opening that doesn't force paper to fold.

## FAQ

### Cross-cut vs micro-cut — what security level do I need?

For personal documents (bank statements, credit card offers, tax returns), P-3 cross-cut is adequate. For sensitive documents (Social Security numbers, business contracts, medical records), go with P-4 micro-cut. P-5 is for government or corporate-level security and is overkill for home offices.

### How long should a shredder run before overheating?

Entry-level models typically run 2-3 minutes. Mid-range models run 10-20 minutes. High-end models run 30-40 minutes. Match the run time to your shredding volume. If you shred a few pages a week, a 3-minute run time is fine. If you shred a whole box of documents quarterly, get a 20+ minute model.

### Can I shred credit cards and staples?

Most shredders handle credit cards and staples. Check the specifications first. But never shred plastic cards in a strip-cut shredder (they can jam the mechanism). Discs and DVDs require specialized shredders and should not go in a standard paper shredder.

### Why does my shredder keep jamming?

Overfilling is the most common cause. Always stay below the rated sheet capacity. Another cause is feeding crumpled or folded paper. The blades catch unevenly. Finally, running the shredder for longer than its rated run time causes the motor to heat up, which reduces torque and increases the chance of jams.
